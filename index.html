Welcome to "America in Miniature!"

The state of Maryland, located in the Mid-Atlantic region on the east coast of the United States is the ninth-smallest state and the fifth most densely populated in the Union. Maryland has several nicknames and earned the monicker "America in Miniature" due to its variety of climates and topography, with the Appalachian mountains in the West, the Piedmont in the Central Region, the Chesapeake bay and Atlantic Ocean on either side of the Eastern Region, swamps in the Southern Region, and plenty of rivers throughout the state.

Here are some fun facts about Maryland:

  • Maryland has five regions: Eastern Shore, Southern, Central, Capital, and Western regions.
  • The flag of Maryland is the heraldic banner of arms of the Cecil, 2nd Baron Baltimore, who founded the Maryland colony in 1632.
  • In 1790 the state of Maryland donated land to the federal government that would later become Washington, D.C.
  • During the War of 1812, Francis Scott Key wrote "The Star Spangled Banner" while the British attempted to capture the city of Baltimore.
